About

Engineer & builder

I'm a second-year EECS student at UC Berkeley, focused on the intersection of embedded systems, signal processing, and intelligent hardware. My work is shaped by a simple belief: the best technology quietly solves a real problem for a real person.

My path into engineering started with physics — mechanics, electromagnetism, and eventually quantum mechanics. That curiosity about how the universe works at every scale still shapes how I approach system design: from first principles, not just from pattern-matching.

At TACC (UT Austin) I built multi-robot coordination systems for disaster response. At Berkeley I'm on the Underwater Robotics electrical subteam, building for a Fung Fellowship project on methane monitoring, and exploring EEG-based attention detection. The throughline: hardware with purpose.

Long-term, I want to work in chip design, system architecture, and AI-powered hardware — building things that are technically deep and genuinely sustainable.
